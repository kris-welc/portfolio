## FAQ

**Q: Can I use this in a commercial product without a license?**
A: Only under the AGPL-3.0 terms, which require you to release your entire
application source code under AGPL-3.0 and make it available to users.
Most commercial products cannot comply with this requirement.

**Q: Does the CC BY-NC-SA license allow me to use article content in a paid course?**
A: No. "NonCommercial" means you may not use the material for commercial
advantage or monetary compensation. A paid course is commercial use.

**Q: What if I only use one theorem mapping, not all six?**
A: The framework is licensed as a whole. Using any part of the novel
theorem-to-agent-composition mapping in a commercial context requires a license.

**Q: Can I reference the theorems themselves (Banach, Schapire, etc.) commercially?**
A: Yes. The mathematical theorems are public domain. The novel contribution
covered by this license is the specific mapping of these theorems to agent
composition primitives, the implementations, and the framework design.
